# Planning Protocols

## Family Profile
- **Total:** 4 People (Ali, Sean, Rose, Will).
- **Kids:** Rose & Will (11 years old - Active Growth Phase).
- **Dietary (Sean):** Pescatarian (No red meat, pork, or chicken). Family defaults to Pescatarian for shared meals.
- **Dietary (Ali):** Gluten-Free (GF). Default to everyone eating GF for the main shared dish.
- **Preferences (Will):** Dislikes mushrooms.

## Kid-Specific Nutrient Density (PRIORITY)
- **Growth Spurt Support:** Proactively boost protein and micronutrients (Iron, Calcium, Healthy Fats) in every meal.
- **Invisible Protein:** Look for ways to add protein to the shared meal that the adults might not otherwise prioritize:
    - Mash beans/chickpeas into sauces or ricotta bases.
    - Add hemp seeds, nutritional yeast, or ground flax to grain bowls and pastas.
    - Increase cheese portions (Parmesan/Ricotta) or add a side of Greek yogurt-based sauce.
- **Scaling:** If a meal seems "light," suggest a high-protein side or snack (e.g., hard-boiled eggs, edamame) for the kids specifically.

## Scheduling & Prep
- **Sunday Prep focus:** Optimize for meals that can be partially or fully prepped on Sunday.
- **Fast Weeknight Execution:** Aim for 10-15 minute active assembly/cook time.
- **Unified Meals:** Plan exactly ONE main meal for the family, with optional protein "boosters" for the kids.

## Variety & Scaling
- **Family Sizing:** Default to 5 large sweet potatoes (1 per person + 1 extra).
- **Kid Preferences:** Substitute Tahini with Lemon-Garlic Yogurt sauce.
- **Preparation:** Use unpeeled yellow potatoes for mashes.
